# Sea and Failure Feedback System

Source file: `Sea_and_Failure_Feedback_System.docx`

Shadows Over Rome – Systems Guide (Sea & Failure Feedback)

This document provides two core systems:1) The Sea as a Living Presence2) The Failure Feedback System (FFS)These systems ensure the world feels reactive, evolving, and immersive.

THE SEA AS A LIVING PRESENCE

The sea is not a creature. It is not an enemy.It is a presence.It behaves consistently, reacts subtly, and becomes increasingly unnaturalas the campaign progresses.IMPORTANT:Never fully explain the sea.

PHASE 1 – BASELINE WRONGNESS

The sea is slightly off, but not dangerous.Use repeatable anomalies:- Waves stop too cleanly at the shore- Water surface is unnaturally smooth in patches- Reflections lag slightly behind movement- Floating objects drift incorrectlyRULE:Repeat the same 2–3 anomalies consistently.GOAL:Players notice patterns, not randomness.

SEA BEHAVIOR PATTERNS

The sea follows consistent “rules”:1) STILLNESSWater becomes perfectly still when not directly observed2) DELAYSplashes and ripples occur slightly after impact3) AWARENESSThe sea reacts to:- Noise- Blood- Certain symbolsGOAL:Players feel the sea is reacting, not just existing.

NPC REACTIONS TO THE SEA

Different NPCs interpret the sea differently:- Fishermen: "It’s wrong. I won’t sail."- Guards: "Just calm water."- Diego: "It’s watching."GOAL:Create tension without confirmation.

ESCALATION (PHASE 2 → 3)

Phase 2:- Shapes under water- Sounds at night- Tide behaving incorrectlyPhase 3:- Water briefly moves against gravity- Reflections show incorrect images- People report seeing things beneath the surfaceGOAL:Transition from unease → fear → horror

FAILURE FEEDBACK SYSTEM (FFS)

The world must react visibly to player actions.Players should not only see consequences at the end,but throughout the campaign.Every meaningful action should create a visible change within 1–2 days.

CORE TRACKING AXES

Track 3 variables:1) MERCHANT POWER (Varro)2) REBELLION STRENGTH3) CULT PROGRESSUse a simple scale:-2 → Weak / disrupted0 → Neutral+2 → Strong / dominant

MERCHANT POWER – FEEDBACK

If players SUPPORT or IGNORE:- More guards in streets- Harsher taxes- Merchants disappear quietly- Official notices increaseIf players OPPOSE:- Guards become uncertain- Conflicting authority appears- Black market grows- Citizens speak more openlyGOAL:Make power visible in everyday life.

REBELLION – FEEDBACK

If players SUPPORT:- Organized camps- Better equipment- Civilian cooperationIf players IGNORE:- Disorganized violence- Looting- DesperationIf players OPPOSE:- Rebels become aggressive- Propaganda increases- Sabotage attempts riseGOAL:Show evolution from movement → force

CULT PROGRESS – FEEDBACK

If players IGNORE:- More disappearances- Symbols spread- Sea anomalies increaseIf players INTERFERE:- Rituals become unstable- Events become unpredictable- Sudden spikes in supernatural activityGOAL:Escalate slowly, then rapidly.

MICRO-FEEDBACK SYSTEM (CRITICAL)

After EVERY quest, ask:"What changes tomorrow?"Examples:- Saved caravan → Market has more goods- Ignored disappearances → Missing posters increase- Helped rebels → Guards suspicious of playersRULE:Always show small consequences quickly.

EVENT 1 – MERCHANT RISE

Consequences:- Economic pressure increases- Food shortages begin- NPC tone becomes cautious and tensePLAYER FEELING:"Something is wrong, but not broken yet"

EVENT 2 – REBELLION SIEGE

Consequences:- Travel restrictions- Escort quests increase- Prices rise sharply- Constant tension in streetsPLAYER FEELING:"We are in the middle of something big"

EVENT 3 – ETERNAL NIGHT

Consequences:- Time becomes unclear- NPC behavior becomes erratic- Creatures appear- Safe zones disappearPLAYER FEELING:"This is out of control"

HOW TO USE THESE SYSTEMS DURING PLAY

At the end of each in-game day:1) Adjust the 3 variables (Merchant / Rebellion / Cult)2) Decide 1–2 visible changes in the world3) Introduce those changes the next dayDuring sessions:- Reuse environmental details (sea behavior)- Show NPC changes over time- Reinforce patternsIMPORTANT:Do NOT explain systems to players.Let them feel the changes naturally.

CORE PRINCIPLE

The world moves with or without the players.But their actions influence HOW it moves.If done correctly, players will feel:"We didn’t just witness the story.""We shaped it."
