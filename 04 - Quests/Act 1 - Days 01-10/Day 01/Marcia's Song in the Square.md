# Marcia's Song in the Square

Board hook: voices wanted for public song - small pay, honest crowd.

This is a light social quest that introduces [[Marcia]] as a bard of the middle class and a beloved figure in the poorer districts. It should feel public, alive, and only lightly political.

## Quest Board Text

Strong voices, capable hands, or stage presence wanted for a public song in the square. No professional experience required. Modest pay, wine included.

Posted by: [[Marcia]]  
Payment: low, plus a free drink.  
Expected duration: 1 hour.

## Step 1 - Meet Marcia

Marcia is near a secondary square, not the richest part of the city. She tunes an instrument and talks with vendors and children as if she knows them all.

Sample lines:

- "New faces. Perfect. Rome listens better to strangers before it knows whether to hate them."
- "I need people who can hold a refrain and not run if someone throws rotten peel."
- "Relax. Today I sing bread and sea. Revolution waits until after lunch."

## Step 2 - Preparation

The players can help by singing, playing, drawing a crowd, handling hecklers, or adding stage tricks.

| Action | Check |
| --- | --- |
| Sing or play well enough | Performance DC 12 |
| Attract a crowd | Persuasion DC 12 |
| Discourage hecklers without chaos | Intimidation DC 13 |
| Add tricks or help collect offerings | Sleight of Hand DC 12 |

## Step 3 - The Song

Marcia sings a popular song about tired sailors, bread becoming too expensive, and masters eating before everyone else. She does not name Varro, the Merchants' Guild, or the government.

Lines to use:

- "When bread rises higher than the sun, someone is holding the ladder."
- "The sea brings fish to those with nets, hunger to those with only hands."
- "Rome laughs with the teeth of the rich and chews with those of the poor."

## Light Conflict

[[Small Merchant Protester]] objects that songs like this put strange ideas in people's heads.

Merchant line:

> "Fine voice, poor judgment. Some things should not be sung in public."

Marcia answer:

> "Then pay me to sing them better."

## Outcomes

- If the players help well, Marcia remembers and greets them personally later.
- If they cause chaos, Marcia laughs but warns that not every fire should be lit on the first day.
- If they ignore the politics, that is fine. This is color, not exposition.

## GM Notes

Do not make Marcia explain Varro. Do not turn this into a manifesto. She should be memorable, not yet informative.

Feeling: "This city already has voices against power, but for now they only sing."
