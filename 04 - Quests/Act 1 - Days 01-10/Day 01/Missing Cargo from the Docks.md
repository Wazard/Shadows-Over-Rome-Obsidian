# Missing Cargo from the Docks

Board hook: cargo missing at the docks - payment on recovery.

This is the opening investigation of [[Act 1 - Varro's Merchant Conspiracy]]. It should look like an ordinary port theft with a few details out of place. Do not reveal [[Gaius Varro]], Aulus, or the cult. The goal is for the party to finish with the feeling that a simple theft almost explains it.

## Quest Board Text

A load unloaded at the port never reached its assigned warehouse. Discreet verification requested near the commercial pier. Payment guaranteed upon confirmation of loss or cargo recovery.

Posted by: Merchants' Guild / port administration.  
Payment: low-good.  
Expected duration: 3-4 hours.

## Locations

- [[Harbor District]]
- area around [[Il Veliero]]
- commercial pier
- unloading zone
- the merchant ship *La Chimera di Giada*

## Step 1 - Port Investigation

The port is chaotic: crates, ropes, sailors, dockworkers, guild workers, bad light, and no single person watching the whole operation.

Useful NPCs:

| NPC | What They Give |
| --- | --- |
| [[Tired Dockworker]] | Confirms the port was busy; knows little else |
| [[Dock Fishmonger]] | Heard night work but treats it as normal port noise |
| [[Official Warehouse Worker]] | Confirms the cargo came from *La Chimera di Giada* and was unloaded after hours |
| [[Crew of the Chimera di Giada]] | Confirms sailors unload, then warehouse staff count and sort |

Important information: sailors do not work fully alone. They unload crates, warehouse staff count them, other workers sort them, and someone assigns them to the correct zones.

## Step 2 - Clue Search

The crew points the players toward the unloading area, then returns to work. Let the players inspect the scene alone.

| Clue | Check | Surface Reading | Hidden Truth |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Wrong unloading area | Investigation or Survival DC 15 | rushed logistics | The cargo was placed where it could be moved toward the sea |
| Stone fragments and deep scratches | Investigation DC 14, DC 16 for stronger read | dropped or dumped cargo | Cargo was dragged to the edge and pushed into the water |
| Burned merchant seals | History/Local DC 10, DC 15 for mismatch | normal guild marks | The marks are normal, but the arrangement is wrong |
| Almost erased charcoal trace | Perception DC 18, Investigation DC 20 | dirt, loading mark, thief sign | A ruined cult-adjacent mark; do not name it as such |
| Broken crate splinters | Perception DC 10, Investigation DC 13 | classic theft evidence | Misdirection that supports the ordinary-theft theory |
| Torn black cloth | Perception DC 13, Investigation/Insight DC 15, History/Local DC 17 | noble, official, rich thief, or false lead | A trace connected to the hooded intermediary, but it should not dominate the scene |
| Drag marks toward a side area | Survival DC 12, DC 16 to see they vanish into normal traffic | movement away from the warehouse | The trail moves toward a less-lit pier area |

Correct Day 1 result: the players can learn that the cargo was moved, likely not by ordinary theft, and maybe pushed into the sea. They cannot yet identify Varro, Aulus, the cult, the hooded man, or the motive.

## Step 3 - Drunk Warehouse Workers

If the players investigate too long or loudly accuse port workers, three drunk warehouse workers confront them.

Tone: suspicious, territorial, hostile to outsiders, not true masterminds.

Sample lines:

- "Are you done sticking your noses in?"
- "This is port business. Go back where you came from."
- "You arrive from Milan and think you understand Rome in one day."

Outcomes:

- Calm talk: they know little and refuse to help.
- Intimidation DC 14: one admits there were unfamiliar faces at the port.
- Persuasion DC 15: one remembers a worker who looked too clean, with hands that lacked calluses.
- Bad escalation: short brawl.

After two rounds of a brawl, [[Man of Aulus]] arrives. He is sober, controlled, and more dangerous. He wants to end the scene, not explain it.

If captured, he can say only that he was sent to make sure nobody made trouble. With Intimidation DC 18, he admits he was there to make sure "the work stayed finished."

## Reward

Full payment if the party investigates, finds at least two meaningful clues, and confirms the cargo is gone.

Reduced payment if they create chaos, assault workers without cause, or return with no evidence beyond "it disappeared."

## GM Notes

Keep the final feeling uneasy, not solved.

What they should think: "This was not a clean theft. Someone moved the cargo, maybe threw it into the sea, and something at the port is off."

What they should not know: Aulus ordered sabotage, Varro is manufacturing scarcity, the cult contaminated the event, nearby sea access matters, or the charcoal mark is cult-related.
