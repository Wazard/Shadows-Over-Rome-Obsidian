# Cult Activity in Act 1

The cult should remain almost invisible in Act 1.

Its job is not to become an investigation yet. Its job is to contaminate existing events with details the players may remember much later.

## Main Rule

The cult is accessible only through [[Diego]] during Act 1. Other clues can appear, but they should feel like atmosphere, superstition, coincidence, or city noise.

Do not let Act 1 become "the cult arc."

## What The Cult Is Doing

- placing small marks in commercial locations already confused by Varro's crisis;
- using cargo, warehouses, sea caves, alleys, and water access points;
- corrupting or convincing minor intermediaries without explaining the larger goal;
- using hand-drawn symbols that can be mistaken for merchant marks;
- watching how the city reacts to scarcity and fear;
- preparing future rituals without making any event look ritualistic.

## What The Cult Should Not Do Yet

- attack the players directly;
- show recognizable cultists;
- leave books, maps, prayers, or names;
- use obvious magic;
- mention eternal night;
- reveal itself as a third plot separate from Varro and the rebellion.

## Clue Tone

Correct Act 1 clues are small, dirty, and easy to ignore:

- a charcoal mark under a crate;
- black cloth in the wrong cargo;
- a candle going out without wind;
- a dog growling toward empty water;
- a fisherman joking that the waves sounded wrong;
- bread tasting faintly of ash.

Incorrect Act 1 clues are too clear:

- complete repeated symbols;
- cult robes;
- open sacrifices;
- obvious necromancy;
- prophecy talk;
- a map to a cult hideout.

## Diego's Role

[[Diego Quest 1]] on day 5 is the first real access point, but it should still look like Diego is rambling. The players should think, "This is probably nonsense, but something about it is off."
