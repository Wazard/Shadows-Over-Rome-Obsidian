# Marcia in Act 1

[[Marcia]] is not yet the leader of the rebellion in Act 1. She is a beloved bard, a social barometer, and a voice for people being squeezed by scarcity.

Her purpose is to show the human cost of the crisis. She should not solve quests for the players or explain Varro's plan. She listens, notices patterns, and gives emotional shape to what the city is feeling.

## Hidden Identity

Marcia is a tiefling woman in her thirties, but she uses a magic ring to appear human. This should not be revealed early. Players should first know her as a person.

Her human appearance is elegant but worn: stylish clothes, poor material, good bearing, and the posture of someone who knows higher rooms than the street.

## Act 1 Trajectory

| Days | Marcia's State | What She Shows |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1-2 | Public singer, charity, listening | Rome is unjust, but this is not new |
| 3-5 | Growing suspicion | Someone is using injustice as a tool |
| 6-7 | Political clarity | Institutions will not save the hungry |
| 8-9 | Organizer emerging | Anger will be organized by someone |
| 10 | Resistance declared or seeded | Varro can be stopped, but the system remains sick |

## Tone

Marcia speaks like a bard, not a clerk. Her words should be memorable, image-rich, and a little dangerous.

Useful lines:

- "I sing for those who pay, but I listen mostly to those who cannot afford it."
- "When the rich are afraid, they call it crisis. When the poor are hungry, they call it habit."
- "A delay is a delay. Two are bad luck. Three are someone counting better than us."
- "The worst men do not smell of alleys. They smell of order."

## Relationship With The Players

If the players show empathy toward workers and poor citizens, Marcia becomes warmer and more useful later.

If they treat the crisis as a source of pay only, she remains polite but cold and classifies them as mercenaries.

If they already suspect Varro, Marcia can confirm the suspicion emotionally, but she should not provide full proof.
