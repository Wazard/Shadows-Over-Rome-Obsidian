# Varro's Plan - Act 1

[[Gaius Varro]] does not want to destroy Rome's economy. He wants to destabilize it, then offer himself as the only person able to restore order.

His control points are:

- distribution: cargo, roads, port routes, warehouse access;
- prices: selective inflation and protected supply;
- access: who gets to trade, travel, report crimes, or receive protection.

## Days 1-2: Invisible Setup

Varro creates instability without exposing himself.

- Aulus Seneca organizes targeted thefts against merchants not loyal to Varro.
- [[Cassian Luro]] buries complaints inside the government.
- Varro hires extra guards for his own caravans so his shipments appear safer and more reliable.
- Varro starts offering protection in exchange for cargo control and a share of sales.

Effect: the city feels disorganized, but no single culprit is obvious.

## Days 3-5: Controlled Instability

Varro expands his network.

- Selective cargo blocks begin.
- Counterfeit coins enter circulation through merchant channels.
- Smaller merchants are pressured with protection money.
- Prices begin to climb, especially on metal goods.
- Lucius Livius and Marcus Livius help connect guild resentment to Varro's plan.

Effect: the market becomes incoherent. Some merchants suffer, while others remain strangely protected.

## Days 6-7: Real Pressure

Varro's influence spreads outside the walls.

- Supplies from nearby settlements are reduced.
- Grain and game imports are cut.
- Merchants loyal to Varro discover that his "protection" is not stable.
- Public anger begins turning toward the Merchants' Guild.

Effect: hunger becomes visible. Varro's system starts hurting even some people who thought they were protected.

## Days 8-9: Public Savior

Varro begins moving from hidden pressure to visible control.

- Pirates reduce the inflow of fish and building materials.
- Guards become more visible in the streets.
- Information is controlled through fear and public messaging.
- Varro appears as a calm noble merchant offering stability.

Effect: the public begins to believe the current government and guild structure cannot solve the crisis.

## Day 10: Collapse or Exposure

If unchecked, many merchants fall under Varro's control. Lucius takes control of the Merchants' Guild, Quintus becomes the only trusted maritime handler for valuable cargo, and the public believes only Varro can save Rome.

If partially checked, control is divided and the market remains unstable.

If exposed, Varro is weakened or forced out of the market narrative, and prices can begin returning toward normal over the next five days.

## Key Agents

| Agent | Role |
| --- | --- |
| [[Cassian Luro]] | Government functionary who hides complaints and gives Varro political cover |
| Aulus Seneca | Slum mercenary, organizes thefts and intimidation |
| Lucius Livius | Second in command of the Merchants' Guild, wants power |
| Marcus Livius | Lucius's brother, common merchant with similar resentments |
| Publius Nerva | Potion and magic goods merchant, joins later |
| Quintus Salvius | Sailor controlling much of Rome's sea traffic |

## Ways Players Can Improve the Ending

- Expose selective pricing and protected routes.
- Protect an independent merchant or route.
- Smasher or expose Cassian, Lucius, Aulus, or Quintus.
- Prevent Varro from becoming the public face of stability.
- Bring proof to [[Il Veliero]], the Merchants' Guild, or another trusted public venue.
