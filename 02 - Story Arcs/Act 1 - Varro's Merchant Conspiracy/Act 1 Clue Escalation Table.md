# Act 1 Clue Escalation Table

This table adds deeper investigation paths around [[Gaius Varro]], [[Aulus Seneca]], and Varro's men without making the conspiracy too easy.

Use the Act 1 rhythm:

- Days 1-3: almost no obvious extra clues beyond quest evidence. The city reads events as festival logistics, bad luck, or petty crime.
- Days 4-6: fewer clean clues inside public quests, but more rumors in the slums, more frightened witnesses, and more purchasable information after black market access opens.
- Days 7-9: investigation meets resistance. Guards, bandits, and Aulus' men interfere if the party digs too aggressively.
- By Days 7-9 the party may know Varro's name as a political and merchant force, but should not have a clean proof trail against him yet.
- Cassian Rulfo should remain hidden unless the players break into a high-level scene or seize paperwork meant to stay private.

## Clue Use

Do not require every clue. A strong Act 1 result should come from patterns:

- 2-3 low clues: something is wrong with cargo and records.
- 2-3 medium clues: someone is organizing paid disruption through intermediaries.
- 1-2 high clues: Aulus' network points upward toward a noble patron.
- Direct Varro proof should require risk: infiltration, captured messenger, black market leverage, or a public mistake by Varro's side.

## Escalation Triggers

Use these when the party investigates hard.

| Trigger | Response |
| --- | --- |
| Party interrogates multiple bandits | Aulus' men move to silence one bandit and relocate payment points |
| Party buys black market info twice | Black market brokers begin charging more and selling news of the party's interest |
| Party publicly names Varro before proof | Varro appears charitable and insulted; public opinion may turn against the party |
| Party enters Noble District in disguise | Guard checks increase next day; uniforms become risky |
| Party fights Aulus' men in the slums | Aulus treats them as active threats and sends better men |
| Party obtains Cassian-adjacent evidence | Official documents become harder to access; Cassian remains hidden unless the party keeps digging |

## Direct Proof Gate

The party should not get direct proof from one lucky check.

To expose Varro cleanly, require at least three categories:

- material evidence: ledgers, tokens, payment tallies, forged permits;
- witness evidence: bandit, courier, guild clerk, servant, or black market broker;
- access evidence: mansion infiltration, courier route, private guild inquiry, or seized official papers;
- public credibility: Marcia, Merchants' Guild, temple witnesses, or respected guards willing to listen.

If they only have one category, they can suspect Varro but cannot force a good ending yet.
