**Shadows over Rome** is a 30-day Pathfinder 1e urban campaign set in [[Rome - Port City]], a large medieval/Roman-inspired coastal city of roughly **40,000 inhabitants**.

The [[players]] arrive as outsiders and slowly become involved in the city’s political collapse, economic manipulation, criminal underworld, rebellion, and finally a hidden apocalyptic cult tied to the sea and eternal darkness.

The campaign uses a **living city structure**: every day, the world changes. The [[Quest Board|quest board]] at [[Il Veliero]] updates, prices shift, shortages worsen, NPCs react, and background events continue even if the players ignore them.

## Campaign Tone

The tone is:

- investigative;
- urban;
- tense;
- morally messy;
- political;
- increasingly supernatural.

At first, the city looks like a corrupt but functional port. Then it becomes clear that something is wrong with the economy. Then that the rebellion is not the only threat. Then, much later, that the real danger has been hiding under everything from the start.

---

# Core Campaign Structure

The campaign has three major hidden arcs, revealed in order:

1. [[Arc 1 - Varro's Merchant Conspiracy]]
2. [[Arc 2 - Marcia's Rebellion]]
3. [[Arc 3 - Aelius and the Cult of Eternal Night]]

These arcs overlap, but they are not the same plot.

[[Gaius Varro]] manipulates the economy.  
[[Marcia]] leads a genuine rebellion.  
[[Aelius Nuurglaag]] serves the deeper apocalyptic threat.

The [[players]] should initially confuse these forces, then slowly separate them through investigation.

# Important Locations

## [[Il Veliero]]

[[Il Veliero]] is the main tavern and player hub. It is located near the docks and warehouse district.

It contains the main [[Quest Board]], which works in a Witcher-style format. The board changes daily and reflects the state of the city.

It is close to:

- [[Harbor District]]
- [[Warehouse District]]
- [[Sea Caves]]
- [[Black Market]] routes, indirectly

## [[Harbor District]]

A large stone port under expansion, full of cranes, ships, dockworkers, sailors, guards, cargo inspections, and suspicious night activity.

Important campaign uses:

- missing shipments;
- silent ships;
- strange markings;
- dockworker disappearances;
- staged cargo thefts;
- cult-adjacent sea clues.

## [[Warehouse District]]

The economic heart of the city.

Important campaign uses:

- grain storage;
- metal shipments;
- merchant ledgers;
- protection money;
- staged thefts;
- merchant fear;
- sabotage connected to [[Gaius Varro]].

The [[Merchants' Guild Headquarters]] stands at the center of this district.

## [[Market Square]]

A large public square below [[Government Hill]], filled with stalls, shops, a central fountain, street performers, and visible signs of price inflation.

The market is useful for showing the city’s condition day by day.

In a good ending, street artists return here.

## [[Noble District]]

An elevated, walled, luxurious area with villas, gardens, walking paths, trees, a theatre, and hidden political meetings.

Some nobles are actually elves using powerful transmutation rings, such as the [[Ring of a Thousand Faces]], to pass as human.

Known linked NPCs:

- [[Cassian Luro]]
- [[Gaius Varro]]
- other hidden elven officials

## [[Government Hill]]

Contains:

- [[Government Palace]]
- [[Temple of Sarenrae]]

This district represents law, state power, official religion, and the illusion of order.

## [[Military District]]

A separately walled area with barracks, training grounds, patrols, armories, and emergency command structures.

It becomes more important as the city destabilizes.

## [[Slums]]

The poorest parts of the city, located around and outside formal protection.

Important campaign uses:

- hunger;
- crime;
- rebel sympathy;
- black-market access;
- desperation;
- moral consequences.

Half-orcs are common here.

## [[Black Market]]

A hidden underworld connected to exotic and marginalized races, including:

- drow;
- tieflings;
- vishkanya;
- other outsiders and criminals.

The black market is ruled by [[Arach-Sul]], a drider crime boss.

It trades with dangerous forces, including the cult, but does not necessarily understand the full scope of [[Aelius Nuurglaag]]’s plan.

# Main Arcs

## [[Arc 1 - Varro's Merchant Conspiracy]]

**Primary phase:** Days 1–10  
**Main antagonist:** [[Gaius Varro]]  
**Theme:** Manufactured scarcity and political capture.

At first, the main problem appears to be economic:

- missing cargo;
- destroyed grain;
- suspicious ledgers;
- protection money;
- staged shortages;
- counterfeit coins;
- corrupt tax enforcement;
- frightened merchants.

The true cause is [[Gaius Varro]], a rich noble merchant and sorcerer who is manufacturing scarcity.

His plan is to collapse public trust in the current government, blame incompetence and corruption, then present himself as the only man capable of fixing the crisis.

Varro is not trying to destroy the city.

He wants to own it.

### Varro’s Tools

- sabotaged shipments;
- staged thefts;
- protection rackets;
- controlled scarcity;
- manipulation of merchants;
- pressure on functionaries;
- false economic panic;
- agents like [[Cassian Luro]].

Important note: [[Gaius Varro]] does **not** know the cult is real. To him, the crisis is political and economic.

---

## [[Arc 2 - Marcia's Rebellion]]

**Primary phase:** Phase 2  
**Main figure:** [[Marcia]]  
**Theme:** Public anger, class conflict, and unstable justice.

As scarcity worsens and public anger grows, the rebellion becomes more visible.

It is led by [[Marcia]], a bard-like figure loved by the middle class, workers, and [[Slums|slums]].

The rebellion is not secretly controlled by [[Gaius Varro]]. This distinction is important.

The [[players]] may think the merchants and rebels are connected, but they are independent forces exploiting the same crisis.

[[Marcia]] represents genuine anger against injustice, but the rebellion can still become violent, unstable, or dangerous depending on how events unfold.

---

## [[Arc 3 - Aelius and the Cult of Eternal Night]]

**Primary phase:** Phase 3  
**Main antagonist:** [[Aelius Nuurglaag|Aelius]]  
**Theme:** Sea, darkness, hidden ritual, apocalypse.

The deepest threat is the cult led by [[Aelius Nuurglaag|Aelius]].

This layer should remain hidden for a long time.

The cult is connected to:

- the sea;
- darkness;
- strange markings;
- missing people;
- [[Sea Caves]];
- black cloth traces;
- whispers;
- [[Diego]]’s paranoia;
- symbols that initially look like meaningless atmosphere.

Their final goal is apocalyptic: an [[Eternal Night]] or catastrophic dark transformation tied to the sea.

During the early days, the cult should not look like the main plot. It should feel like background weirdness.

# Political Structure

Main note: [[Government Palace|City government]]

The city government is oligarchic. The governor is elected by functionaries, and functionaries are elected through wealth-weighted representation.

Possible seat model:

| Class        | Seats |
| ------------ | ----: |
| Nobles       |    12 |
| Merchants    |     5 |
| Middle Class |     2 |
| Poor         |     1 |

This makes the system technically representative but deeply biased toward wealth.

[[Gaius Varro|Varro]]’s plan depends on manipulating this structure rather than openly overthrowing it.

He wants enough noble and merchant support, plus public panic, to force a no-confidence crisis or functional transfer of power.

# Clue Philosophy

Main note: [[Clue Design Philosophy]]

The campaign should not railroad the players into one obvious plot.

Instead, it works through accumulation.

At first, each quest seems isolated.

Then the players notice repeated details:

- missing cargo;
- grain shortages;
- strange payments;
- merchant fear;
- tax pressure;
- warehouse sabotage;
- night unloading;
- black cloth;
- symbols;
- people being silenced.

Then they realize there are multiple plots overlapping.

## Important Design Rule

Not every clue matters.

Some details are just city noise:

- rats;
- broken bottles;
- strange scratches;
- meaningless marks;
- distant arguments;
- weird smells;
- loose tiles;
- random noises.

This prevents players from assuming every atmospheric detail is part of the conspiracy.

See also: [[Meaningless Atmosphere Table]]

# Endgame Logic

Main note: [[Campaign Endings]]

The campaign has several possible outcomes depending on what the players uncover, who they support, and what they prevent.

## Bad Ending

The city collapses into despair, violence, religious dependency, crime, and possibly darkness.

The [[Slums|slums]] become more dangerous, the population suffers, and the real threat may triumph.

## Mid Ending

The worst is prevented, but the city remains damaged.

The rebellion, merchant conspiracy, or cult may be only partially resolved.

Some corruption survives.

## Good Ending

The city stabilizes.

Slum crime returns to baseline, public life recovers, the market becomes lively again, street artists return, and the city feels wounded but alive.

# Campaign Identity

**Shadows over Rome** is a political-investigative urban Pathfinder campaign where the players begin as outsiders taking jobs from a tavern board, only to uncover a manufactured economic collapse, a rising rebellion, a criminal underworld, hidden nonhuman elites, and finally a sea-linked cult trying to bring [[Eternal Night|eternal night]] over Rome.

The strongest part of the campaign is the layered structure:

- [[Gaius Varro|Varro]] looks like the main villain.
- [[Marcia]] looks like the dangerous consequence.
- [[Aelius Nuurglaag|Aelius]] is the real shadow behind the city’s slow descent.
