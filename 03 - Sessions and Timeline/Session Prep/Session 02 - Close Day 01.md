# Session 02 - Close Day 01

## Starting Position

- In-game time: [[Day 01]], Desnus 1, 3608, 13:00.
- Party location: [[Government Palace]].
- Current permission: the party has permits to re-enter Rome at night.
- Completed this morning:
  - [[Marcia's Song in the Square]], completed without fighting.
  - [[Parcel to the Apothecary]], delivered intact.
- The parcel was not opened.
- The party did not uncover who the apothecary is.

## Session Goal

Close [[Day 01]] with one meaningful afternoon/evening investigation, one Breaching Festival city-mood beat, and a night re-entry moment that makes the permits feel useful.

Do not reveal [[Gaius Varro]], [[Aulus Seneca]], or the cult as a solved plot. The end-of-day feeling should be:

> Rome is celebrating beautifully, but ordinary systems are beginning to grind beneath the music.

## Breaching Festival Frame

Desnus 1 is the public opening day of the Breaching Festival season.

- Afternoon: the whole city seems to be outside preparing garlands, market stalls, street stages, lanterns, temporary food stands, and harbor viewing spots.
- [[Il Veliero]] is preparing a special dinner service; it can be warm, crowded, and useful as a social hub.
- Harbor fireworks are expected after dark, giving the evening a strong reason to move toward the water.
- The party's night re-entry permits matter because crowds, fireworks, and festival traffic make gates and checkpoints stricter after sunset.
- Keep the contrast visible: public joy on the surface, missing cargo and bad records underneath.

## Opening Scene - Government Palace

The permit office is efficient but indifferent. The papers are real, stamped, and annoying.

Use this to show legal order, not corruption yet.

Details:

- The clerk tells them the permit allows night re-entry, not immunity from searches.
- A guard warns them that after dark, gates open slowly and questions multiply.
- A junior clerk complains that too many small disputes are reaching the palace today.
- Outside, festival decorators are already blocking sightlines and making every official errand slower.

Lines:

- "Stamped. Fold it once, not twice. If the seal cracks, the gate captain will enjoy making that your problem."
- "Night entry means you may ask to enter. It does not mean the gate owes you speed."
- "Cargo complaints, market complaints, road complaints. If Rome could complain on parchment, it would."
- "Enjoy the fireworks. Everyone else will. That is why the gates will be miserable."

Optional names:

- [[Cassian Rulfo]] is not personally present.
- A clerk can mention that Cassian's office is busy with commercial petitions.

## Choice Point

Let the party choose, but frame the remaining Day 1 board clearly.

Strong default:

- [[Missing Cargo from the Docks]] if they want the main Act 1 thread.

Good alternatives:

- [[Lost Ledger near the Harbor]] if they want a shorter investigation.
- [[Wolves Outside the Walls]] if they want to use the night re-entry permits and end outside the walls.

If the players drift, have [[Il Veliero]] staff or a government clerk remind them that the cargo and ledger jobs are still open and near the harbor.

Festival push:

- [[Missing Cargo from the Docks]] is easiest to pitch because the harbor is already where people will gather for fireworks.
- [[Lost Ledger near the Harbor]] works if they want something smaller before dinner at [[Il Veliero]].
- [[Wolves Outside the Walls]] lets the permits matter, but it should contrast with the city celebration rather than replace it.

### Running Them

- Keep these short: 5-10 minutes each.
- Do not reveal a culprit.
- Give one clue, one mood beat, and one reason to move.
- If players follow the clue immediately, transition into the matching main job.
- If they ignore it, let the city pressure remain as background evidence for later.

## Main Job Option A - Missing Cargo From The Docks

Best fit for the session goal.

Run only the spine:

1. Reach the [[Harbor District]] and identify the commercial pier.
2. Speak with [[Tired Dockworker]], [[Official Warehouse Worker]], or [[Crew of the Chimera di Giada]].
3. Inspect the side unloading area.
4. Find at least two meaningful clues.
5. End with the sense that the cargo was moved toward the water, not simply stolen.

Important clues to prioritize:

| Clue | Check | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Wrong unloading area | Investigation or Survival DC 15 | Cargo was placed where later movement was easy |
| Stone fragments and deep scratches | Investigation DC 14 | Something heavy was dragged or dumped |
| Drag marks toward side pier | Survival DC 12 / DC 16 | Trail vanishes into ordinary traffic |
| Burned merchant seals | History/Local DC 10 / DC 15 | Normal marks, wrong arrangement |
| Almost erased charcoal trace | Perception DC 18 / Investigation DC 20 | Strange background noise; do not explain |

Complication:

- Three drunk warehouse workers confront them if the investigation gets loud.
- Resolve socially if possible.
- If a fight starts, keep it short. After two rounds, [[Man of Aulus]] arrives to stop the mess, not confess.

Outcome:

- They can confirm the cargo is gone and probably moved to water.
- They cannot identify the mastermind.

## Main Job Option B - Lost Ledger Near The Harbor

Use if the table wants something more contained.

Run only the spine:

1. Meet [[Publius - Apprentice]] near [[Il Veliero]] or the harbor.
2. Reconstruct his route.
3. Search the water-side area.
4. Recover the damaged ledger or enough fragments to worry him.

Important beats:

- Publius is not a mastermind; he is frightened and minimizing the danger.
- If they found the ledger, he is relieved that it is damaged.
- If they copy fragments, they have a future comparison tool for cargo discrepancies.

Useful clues:

- Diluted ink stains.
- Torn page with cargo discrepancy fragment.
- Almost unreadable future cargo notes.
- Publius avoiding the water detail.

Outcome:

- The party learns that records are already wrong on Day 1.
- Keep the culprit unclear.

## Main Job Option C - Wolves Outside The Walls

Use if they want to test the night permits and leave the city.

Run it as a normal problem.

Structure:

1. Meet [[Faustus]] near a secondary gate.
2. Leave Rome in late afternoon.
3. Inspect the flock and weak enclosure.
4. Drive off or fight the wolves near sunset.
5. Return after dark using the permits.

Important:

- No cult signs.
- No Varro signs.
- No hidden conspiracy.

This quest is useful because it proves not every problem is secretly connected.

## Evening City Beats

Pick one after the main job.

### Marcia In The Square

Use if the players return through [[Market Square]].

[[Marcia]] is performing again, looser than this morning. She recognizes the party if they helped her.

Line:

> "My brave choir returns. No rotten peel in your hair. A professional success."

She can mention that people are talking about delayed cargo and nervous dockworkers, but she does not know why.

### Market Unease

Use if they pass the market.

Borrow from [[Day 01 - Sera - Market Square - Marcia]] or [[Day 01 - Sera - Merchant District - Orefice Tullia]]:

- People joke about high prices, but the jokes have teeth.
- A vendor refuses worn coins.
- Someone says port delays are becoming everyone's excuse.

### Temple Pressure

Use if they pass [[Temple of Sarenrae]].

Borrow from [[Day 01 - Sera - Temple of Sarenrae - Sorella Elenia]]:

- A worker injured at the port is being treated.
- The temple asks for quiet, not justice.
- This reinforces the dock situation without turning it into exposition.

## Night Re-Entry Beat

Use even if they never leave Rome: the permits should matter by the end.

Best if they did [[Wolves Outside the Walls]]:

- The party reaches a gate after dark.
- The guard inspects the seal carefully.
- Another tired guard mutters that people are trying to enter with cargo excuses all night.
- The permit works, but the process is slow and humiliating.

If they stayed inside Rome:

- A guard checkpoint asks for papers while they move through a controlled street near the harbor or government district.
- The permit prevents delay but draws attention.

Line:

> "You may pass. Next time, arrive before the lamps are lit and save everyone the paperwork."

## Closing Day 1

End with a quiet city image, not a cliffhanger explosion.

Choose one:

- From a high street, the party sees festival lanterns and firework crews moving near the harbor after normal work hours.
- At [[Il Veliero]], someone adds two more work notices to the board before closing.
- A dockworker drinks alone and says, "Tomorrow will be clearer." He does not sound convinced.
- Fireworks bloom over the harbor; somewhere below the cheers, a rope creaks, then goes still.

## Day 1 End State

Canon after this session unless play changes it:

- [[Marcia]] likes or at least remembers the party.
- The apothecary parcel was delivered intact; its contents and recipient remain unexplored.
- The party has valid night re-entry permits.
- If [[Missing Cargo from the Docks]] is completed, the party knows the cargo disappearance was staged or manipulated, but has no culprit.
- If [[Lost Ledger near the Harbor]] is completed, the party has seen record discrepancies, but not the full pattern.
- If [[Wolves Outside the Walls]] is completed, Rome still has ordinary problems outside the conspiracy.

## Do Not Reveal Yet

- [[Gaius Varro]]'s role.
- [[Aulus Seneca]] as an organizer unless the party forces a confrontation through [[Man of Aulus]].
- Cult meaning behind any charcoal, water, candle, or darkness clue.
- The apothecary's identity or importance unless the players actively return to that thread.
