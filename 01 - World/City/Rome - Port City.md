# Rome - Port City

Rome is a Roman-style coastal metropolis divided into districts with different pressures, loyalties, and campaign uses.

## Major Districts

| District | Function | Main Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [[Il Veliero]] | Player hub and quest board | Tavern near the docks |
| [[Harbor District]] | Ships, cargo, dockworkers, sea access | Missing cargo, sabotage, strange arrivals |
| [[Warehouse District]] | Economic heart of the city | Grain, ledgers, guild politics, sabotage |
| [[Merchant District]] | Merchants' Guild and laboratories | Guild politics, controlled routes, merchant NPCs |
| [[Market Square]] | Public economy and city mood | Inflation, food scarcity, performers, riots |
| [[Noble District]] | Wealth, villas, hidden elven nobles | Luxury, intrigue, transmutation rings |
| [[Government Hill]] | State power and religion | [[Government Palace]] and [[Temple of Sarenrae]] |
| [[Military District]] | Soldiers, barracks, training grounds | Order, repression, siege response |
| [[Slums]] | Poverty, crime, rebel sympathy | Hunger, desperation, street politics |
| [[Black Market]] | Criminal underworld | Ruled by [[Arach-Sul]] |

## Act 1 Use

In [[Act 1 - Varro's Merchant Conspiracy]], Rome should feel functional but increasingly wrong. The port is busy, the market is still alive, and the government still looks stable, but logistics begin to fail in ways that benefit [[Gaius Varro]].

The city mood should move from inconvenience to suspicion to fear:

- Days 1-2: missing cargo, small delays, normal market surface.
- Days 3-5: price pressure, counterfeit coins, merchant intimidation.
- Days 6-7: visible scarcity, public anger, routes becoming unsafe.
- Days 8-10: Varro presents himself as the only source of order.
